# Create your first flowchart in FigJam

## Introduction

This task will show you how to create a simple flowchart in FigJam. You will place shapes, label each step, and connect them in the correct order to show how a process works. After you complete this task, you will have a finished beginner flowchart that you can use as a model for future diagrams.

## Procedure

1. **Open** a blank **FigJam** board or continue on an empty area of your existing board.

![Open a blank area in a FigJam board](../assets/Task2/Img1.png "Open a blank area in a FigJam board")

Starting with a clear area makes it easier to organize the flowchart and prevents shapes from overlapping with other content.

!!! success
    You should have enough empty space to place several shapes vertically.

2. **Insert** the shapes for this process: a rounded shape for `Start`, rectangles for `Open assignment`, `Finish work`, and `Submit assignment`, a diamond for `Work complete?`, and a rounded shape for `End`.

![Place the flowchart shapes on the board](../assets/Task2/Img2.png "Place the flowchart shapes on the board")

Different shapes help readers understand the role of each step. In a basic flowchart, process steps are usually rectangles, while decisions are shown with a diamond.

!!! success
    You should see all six shapes on the board.

!!! note
    The exact size of each shape does not matter, but leave enough room for labels and connectors.

3. **Double-click** each shape and **type** its label: `Start`, `Open assignment`, `Finish work`, `Work complete?`, `Submit assignment`, and `End`.

![Add labels to each flowchart shape](../assets/Task2/Img3.png "Add labels to each flowchart shape")

Labels explain what each step means. Without labels, the reader can see the shapes but cannot understand the process.

!!! success
    Every shape should now contain short, readable text.

4. **Move** the shapes into order from top to bottom. Place `Start`, `Open assignment`, `Finish work`, and `Work complete?` in one vertical line. Place `Submit assignment` to the right of the decision shape, and place `End` below it.

![Arrange the shapes into a readable flowchart layout](../assets/Task2/Img4.png "Arrange the shapes into a readable flowchart layout")

A clear layout helps the reader follow the process from beginning to end. Placing the decision in the middle makes the branch easier to understand.

!!! success
    Your flowchart should now look organized, with a clear direction of movement.

5. **Click** the **Connector** tool and **connect** the shapes in this order: `Start` → `Open assignment` → `Finish work` → `Work complete?`. Then connect the `Yes` branch to `Submit assignment`, connect `Submit assignment` to `End`, and connect the `No` branch back to `Finish work`.

![Connect the shapes with arrows](../assets/Task2/Img5.png "Connect the shapes with arrows")

Connectors show how the user moves through the process. The branch back to `Finish work` shows that the task continues until the work is complete.

!!! success
    You should now see arrows connecting all related shapes.

!!! warning
    Check that each arrow points to the correct next step. One connector in the wrong direction can change the meaning of the entire flowchart.

6. **Add** small `Yes` and `No` labels beside the two decision branches, then **align** the shapes and connectors so the chart is easy to read.

![Add Yes and No labels and align the finished flowchart](../assets/Task2/Img6.png "Add Yes and No labels and align the finished flowchart")

Branch labels tell the reader which path to follow after the decision. Final alignment makes the chart cleaner and more professional.

!!! success
    Your finished flowchart should be neat, readable, and fully connected.

## Conclusion

You have created your first flowchart in FigJam by placing shapes, adding labels, and connecting each step in order. This type of diagram is useful for showing how a process works and where decisions change the path. You can now use the same method to build more detailed flowcharts for class, planning, or group work.

For help with missing connectors, overlapping shapes, or unreadable labels, see [Troubleshooting](troubleshooting.md).
